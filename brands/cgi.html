Overview

CGI Windows & Doors was founded in 1992, the same year Hurricane Andrew devastated South Florida and triggered the most significant building code reforms in state history. As Florida mandated impact-resistant glazing in the High Velocity Hurricane Zone, CGI emerged as one of the pioneering manufacturers meeting the new standards.

In 1994, CGI became one of the first manufacturers to offer laminated impact glass approved under Miami-Dade County protocols — a landmark achievement at a time when the impact window industry was still being defined. For three decades, CGI built a reputation for architectural-grade aluminum windows and doors serving South Florida's residential and light commercial markets.

Product families including Sentinel, Estate, and Sparta became familiar names on Florida job sites. CGI's focus on clean sightlines, durable aluminum frames, and rigorous impact testing made it a preferred specification for builders and architects in coastal counties.

CGI joined the PGT Innovations family of brands in 2014 and now operates within the Miter Brands portfolio. AOA Window Solutions continues to offer CGI product lines and legacy systems, helping homeowners and property owners access proven impact-rated solutions backed by decades of Florida hurricane performance.

Manufacturing & operations

Historically manufactured in the Miami area (Hialeah/Medley), serving South Florida and statewide impact markets. Now part of the broader PGT Innovations / Miter Brands manufacturing network.

Florida & coastal performance

CGI was among the first manufacturers to achieve Miami-Dade County impact approval after Hurricane Andrew — helping define what a hurricane window needed to be in the 1990s.
